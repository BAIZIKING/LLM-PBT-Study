1. The result is always a `Decimal` instance.

2. If either operand is `NaN`, the result is `Decimal('NaN')`.

3. If neither operand is `NaN`, the result is one of exactly `Decimal('-1')`, `Decimal('0')`, or `Decimal('1')`.

4. If neither operand is `NaN`, the result is `Decimal('-1')` exactly when the first operand is numerically less than the second operand, `Decimal('0')` exactly when they are numerically equal, and `Decimal('1')` exactly when the first operand is numerically greater.

5. For non-`NaN` operands, reversing the operands negates the result: `a.compare(b) == -b.compare(a)`.
