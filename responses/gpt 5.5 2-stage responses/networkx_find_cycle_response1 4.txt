1. If the function returns normally, the returned value is a non-empty list of edge tuples.

2. Every returned edge tuple corresponds to an edge that actually exists in `G`; for multigraphs, the returned key identifies an existing parallel edge.

3. The returned edges form a closed cyclic path: the traversal head of each edge is the traversal tail of the next edge, and the traversal head of the last edge is the traversal tail of the first edge.

4. The shape of each returned edge tuple matches the graph type and `orientation`: `(u, v)` for graphs, `(u, v, key)` for multigraphs, with an additional final direction entry `'forward'` or `'reverse'` whenever `orientation` is not `None`.

5. For directed graphs, the returned traversal directions are consistent with `orientation`: `None`/`'original'` only follows edge direction, `'reverse'` follows edges opposite their direction, and `'ignore'` may traverse each directed edge in either direction while reporting the chosen direction.
