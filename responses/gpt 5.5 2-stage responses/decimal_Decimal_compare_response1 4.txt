1. If either operand is a NaN, the result is a Decimal NaN.

2. If neither operand is NaN, the result is always one of exactly `Decimal('-1')`, `Decimal('0')`, or `Decimal('1')`.

3. For non-NaN operands, the result is `Decimal('-1')` exactly when the first operand is numerically less than the second.

4. For non-NaN operands, the result is `Decimal('0')` exactly when the two operands are numerically equal.

5. For non-NaN operands, swapping the operands negates the comparison result: `a.compare(b) == -b.compare(a)`.
