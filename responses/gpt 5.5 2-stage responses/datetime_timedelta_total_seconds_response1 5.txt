1. The output is a `float` representing the duration in seconds.

2. For any `timedelta` `td`, the output equals `td / timedelta(seconds=1)`.

3. For any `timedelta` `td`, the output equals `td.days * 86400 + td.seconds + td.microseconds / 1_000_000`, subject to floating-point precision limits.

4. A zero-duration `timedelta` always returns `0.0`.

5. If two `timedelta` objects are equal after normalization, their `total_seconds()` outputs are equal.
