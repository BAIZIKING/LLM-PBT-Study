1. The result has three named components: `year`, `week`, and `weekday`.

2. `weekday` is always an integer from `1` to `7`, where Monday is `1` and Sunday is `7`.

3. `week` is always an integer from `1` to `53`.

4. The ISO `year` is always either the same as the Gregorian year of the input date, the previous year, or the next year.

5. Reconstructing the date from the returned ISO values should produce the original date: `date.fromisocalendar(year, week, weekday)` equals the input date.
