1. For every edge `(u, v)` in the input graph, the returned `flow_dict[u][v]` is nonnegative and does not exceed the edge capacity; if the capacity attribute is missing, the flow is finite unless an unbounded-flow exception is raised.

2. For every node other than the source `_s` and sink `_t`, flow is conserved: the total incoming flow equals the total outgoing flow.

3. The returned `flow_value` equals the net flow out of the source: total flow leaving `_s` minus total flow entering `_s`.

4. The returned `flow_value` also equals the net flow into the sink: total flow entering `_t` minus total flow leaving `_t`.

5. The returned flow is maximal: its `flow_value` equals the capacity of a minimum `_s`-`_t` cut in the same graph.
