1. For any iterable with at least two real-valued numbers, `variance(data)` is always non-negative.

2. `variance(data)` equals `sum((x - mean(data)) ** 2 for x in data) / (len(data) - 1)`.

3. If all values in `data` are identical, `variance(data)` returns zero.

4. Adding the same constant to every value in `data` does not change the variance.

5. Multiplying every value in `data` by a constant `k` multiplies the variance by `k ** 2`.
