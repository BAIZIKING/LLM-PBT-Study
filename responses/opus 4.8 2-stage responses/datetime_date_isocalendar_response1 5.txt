1. The output is a named tuple with exactly three components: year, week, and weekday.

2. The week component is always an integer between 1 and 53 inclusive (since an ISO year consists of 52 or 53 weeks).

3. The weekday component is always an integer between 1 and 7 inclusive (Monday=1 through Sunday=7).

4. The ISO year component is always within one year of the date's Gregorian year (i.e., it equals the Gregorian year, or the year before, or the year after).

5. Two dates that are exactly 7 days apart yield the same weekday, and consecutive dates yield weekdays that cycle through 1–7 (incrementing by 1, wrapping from 7 back to 1).
