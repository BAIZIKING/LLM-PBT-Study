1. The mean of a list is always greater than or equal to the minimum element and less than or equal to the maximum element of the data.

2. Adding a constant value to every element in the data increases the mean by exactly that constant value.

3. Multiplying every element in the data by a constant factor multiplies the mean by that same factor.

4. The mean of a list containing a single repeated value (or any list where all elements are equal) equals that value.

5. The mean multiplied by the number of data points equals the sum of all the data points.
