1. The exponent of the result is always equal to the exponent of the second operand (`exp`), unless an error condition (such as InvalidOperation) is signaled.

2. The numeric value of the result equals the numeric value of the first operand after rounding to the precision determined by `exp` (i.e., the result is the closest representable value at that exponent according to the rounding mode).

3. If the second operand's exponent is greater than or equal to the first operand's exponent, no rounding can change the value beyond truncation/rounding to that exponent, and the result's value differs from the first operand by less than one unit in the place of `exp`.

4. When the second operand's exponent is less than or equal to the first operand's exponent (no rounding needed), the result is numerically exactly equal to the first operand.

5. If the number of significant digits in the result's coefficient would exceed the context's precision, or the resulting exponent is greater than Emax or less than Etiny(), an InvalidOperation error is signaled rather than a normal Decimal value being returned.
