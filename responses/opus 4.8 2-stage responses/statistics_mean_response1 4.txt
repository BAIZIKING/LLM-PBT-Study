1. The mean of the data is always greater than or equal to the minimum value and less than or equal to the maximum value in the data.

2. The mean of a list containing a single repeated value (or just one element) equals that value.

3. Adding a constant c to every element in the data increases the mean by exactly c (translation invariance).

4. Multiplying every element in the data by a constant k multiplies the mean by exactly k (scaling).

5. The sum of all data points equals the mean multiplied by the number of data points.
