1. The exponent of the result is always equal to the exponent of the second operand (`exp`), provided no error condition (e.g., InvalidOperation) is raised.

2. The result is numerically equal to the first operand rounded to the precision implied by `exp`'s exponent; in particular, if the first operand already has an exponent greater than or equal to `exp`'s exponent and fits within precision, the result equals the first operand in value.

3. The absolute difference between the result and the original first operand is at most one unit in the last place of `exp` (i.e., bounded by the magnitude of `exp`'s exponent), reflecting that only rounding up to the target exponent occurs.

4. An InvalidOperation is signaled whenever the coefficient length of the quantized result would exceed the context's precision, or whenever the resulting exponent would be greater than Emax or less than Etiny().

5. The operation never signals Underflow, even when the result is subnormal and inexact (i.e., quantize succeeds on subnormal results that other operations might flag).
