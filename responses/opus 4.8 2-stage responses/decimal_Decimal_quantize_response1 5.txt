1. The exponent of the result is always equal to the exponent of the second operand (`exp`), unless an error condition (InvalidOperation) is signaled.

2. The numeric value of the result is the value of the first operand rounded to the precision implied by `exp`, so the result differs from the first operand by less than one unit in the last place of `exp`.

3. When the exponent of `exp` is less than or equal to the exponent of the first operand (no rounding needed), the result is numerically equal to the first operand.

4. The length of the coefficient of the result never exceeds the context precision; if it would, an InvalidOperation is raised instead of returning a value.

5. An error (InvalidOperation) is signaled whenever the resulting exponent would be greater than Emax or less than Etiny(), and Underflow is never signaled even for subnormal inexact results.
