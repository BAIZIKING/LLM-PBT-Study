1. The maximum flow value must equal the net outflow from the source, which equals the net inflow into the sink (i.e., total flow leaving `_s` equals total flow entering `_t` equals `flow_value`).

2. Flow conservation must hold at every node other than the source and sink: for each such node, the sum of incoming flows equals the sum of outgoing flows.

3. The flow on every edge must not exceed that edge's capacity (and must be non-negative), respecting the capacity constraint for all edges in `flow_dict`.

4. The returned `flow_value` must be non-negative, and it must equal the sum of the flows over all edges in `flow_dict` originating at the source `_s`.

5. The `flow_value` computed should be invariant with respect to the choice of `flow_func` (e.g., the default, `shortest_augmenting_path`, `edmonds_karp`, and `preflow_push` should all yield the same maximum flow value for the same graph, source, and sink).
