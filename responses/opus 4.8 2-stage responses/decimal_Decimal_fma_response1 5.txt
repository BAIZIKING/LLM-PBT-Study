1. The result of `self.fma(other, third)` equals `self*other + third` when the intermediate product does not require rounding (i.e., for exact arithmetic cases, the output matches direct computation).

2. The output is mathematically equivalent to multiplying self by other (without intermediate rounding) and then adding third, which may differ from `(self * other) + third` only in cases where the intermediate product would otherwise be rounded.

3. When `third` is zero, the result of `self.fma(other, 0)` equals the unrounded product of self and other (subject only to final rounding from addition).

4. When `other` is one, the result of `self.fma(1, third)` equals `self + third`.

5. The fma operation is commutative in its first two arguments: `self.fma(other, third)` equals `other.fma(self, third)`.
