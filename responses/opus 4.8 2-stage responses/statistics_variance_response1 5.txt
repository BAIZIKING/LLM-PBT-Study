1. The variance is always non-negative (greater than or equal to zero) for any valid input data.

2. The variance equals zero if and only if all data points are identical (constant data).

3. The variance is invariant under translation: adding a constant to every data point does not change the variance.

4. The variance scales by the square of a multiplicative factor: multiplying every data point by a constant `c` multiplies the variance by `c²`.

5. Passing the correctly computed mean as the optional `xbar` argument yields the same result as omitting it (when the function computes the mean automatically).
