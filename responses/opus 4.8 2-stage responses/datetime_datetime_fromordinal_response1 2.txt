1. The returned object is a `datetime` instance whose hour, minute, second, and microsecond are all 0.

2. The returned datetime's `tzinfo` is None.

3. Calling `.toordinal()` on the returned datetime yields back the original input ordinal (round-trip consistency).

4. For inputs outside the valid range (ordinal < 1 or ordinal > datetime.max.toordinal()), a ValueError is raised.

5. The result is monotonically increasing with the input: a larger ordinal produces a datetime that is strictly later (greater) than that produced by a smaller ordinal, with consecutive ordinals differing by exactly one day.
