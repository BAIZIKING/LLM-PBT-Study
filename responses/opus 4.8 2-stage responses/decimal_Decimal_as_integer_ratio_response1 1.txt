1. The denominator `d` is always a positive integer (strictly greater than 0).

2. The fraction `n/d` is in lowest terms, meaning the greatest common divisor of `n` and `d` is 1.

3. The ratio is exact: reconstructing a Decimal from `n` divided by `d` (e.g., via `Decimal(n) / Decimal(d)`) equals the original Decimal value.

4. The sign of the numerator `n` matches the sign of the original Decimal (negative for negative values, zero for zero, positive for positive values).

5. Calling the function on an infinity raises an OverflowError, and calling it on a NaN raises a ValueError.
