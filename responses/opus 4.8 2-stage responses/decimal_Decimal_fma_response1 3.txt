1. The result of `self.fma(other, third)` equals `self * other + third` when the intermediate product does not require rounding (i.e., exact arithmetic), matching the mathematical definition.

2. The result equals the value obtained by performing the multiplication and addition with full precision on the intermediate product, then rounding only once at the end — which may differ from `(self * other) + third` when the separate multiplication would have introduced rounding.

3. When `third` is zero, the result equals `self * other` (with the same single rounding behavior as multiplication of self and other).

4. When `self` or `other` is one, the result equals `third + other` or `third + self` respectively (the multiplication acts as identity on the other operand).

5. The operation is commutative in the first two arguments: `self.fma(other, third)` equals `other.fma(self, third)` for the multiplicative part.
