Here are five properties of the output of `datetime.fromordinal(ordinal)` suitable for property-based testing:

1. The hour, minute, second, and microsecond of the returned datetime are all 0.

2. The `tzinfo` of the returned datetime is None.

3. Calling `.toordinal()` on the returned datetime yields back the original input ordinal (round-trip consistency).

4. The returned object is an instance of `datetime.datetime`, and its year, month, and day are valid (within their respective allowed ranges).

5. For inputs outside the valid range (i.e., ordinal < 1 or ordinal > datetime.max.toordinal()), a ValueError is raised instead of returning a datetime.
