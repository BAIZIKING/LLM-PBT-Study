1. The mean of the data is always greater than or equal to the minimum value and less than or equal to the maximum value of the data (i.e., it lies within the range of the input).

2. The mean of a list containing a single repeated value (or a single element) equals that value.

3. Adding a constant `c` to every element in the data increases the mean by exactly `c` (translation invariance).

4. Multiplying every element in the data by a constant `k` multiplies the mean by exactly `k` (scaling property).

5. The mean multiplied by the number of data points equals the sum of all the data points.
