1. The output is always a non-negative float (specifically, a positive float for valid input, since all values must be positive).

2. For a dataset where all elements are equal to some value c, the geometric mean equals c.

3. The geometric mean is always bounded between the minimum and maximum values of the input data (inclusive).

4. Multiplying every element in the data by a positive constant k multiplies the geometric mean by exactly k (scaling property).

5. The geometric mean is less than or equal to the arithmetic mean of the same dataset (AM-GM inequality).
