1. The output is a named tuple with exactly three components named `year`, `week`, and `weekday`.

2. The `week` component is always an integer between 1 and 53 (inclusive).

3. The `weekday` component is always an integer between 1 and 7 (inclusive), where 1 represents Monday and 7 represents Sunday.

4. The `year` component is within 1 of the date's Gregorian year (it can equal the Gregorian year, or be one less near the start of January, or one more near the end of December).

5. The `weekday` component is consistent with the date's actual day of the week (i.e., it equals the Gregorian weekday + 1, matching `date.weekday() + 1` or equivalently `date.isoweekday()`).
