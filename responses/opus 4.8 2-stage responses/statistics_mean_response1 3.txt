1. The mean of the data is always greater than or equal to the minimum value and less than or equal to the maximum value of the data.

2. The mean of a list containing a single repeated value (or a single element) equals that value.

3. Adding a constant c to every element of the data increases the mean by exactly c.

4. Multiplying every element of the data by a constant c multiplies the mean by exactly c.

5. The mean multiplied by the number of data points equals the sum of all the data points.
