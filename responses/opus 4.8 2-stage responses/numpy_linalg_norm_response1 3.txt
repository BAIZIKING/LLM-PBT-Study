1. **Non-negativity for standard norms**: For any input `x` with `ord` being a norm order ≥ 1 (or `None`, `'fro'`, `'nuc'`, `inf`), the output is always non-negative (≥ 0).

2. **Absolute homogeneity (scaling)**: For a scalar `s`, the norm of `s * x` equals `abs(s)` times the norm of `x`, i.e., `norm(s*x, ord) == abs(s) * norm(x, ord)` for valid norm orders.

3. **Zero norm of zero vector**: For any valid `ord` (where the norm is well-defined), the norm of an all-zeros array equals 0.

4. **Triangle inequality**: For valid norm orders (≥ 1), the norm of a sum is at most the sum of norms, i.e., `norm(x + y, ord) <= norm(x, ord) + norm(y, ord)`.

5. **Equivalence of default vector norm to Euclidean (2-norm)**: For a 1-D array with `ord=None`, the output equals `sqrt(sum(abs(x)**2))`, and equals `norm(x, 2)`.
