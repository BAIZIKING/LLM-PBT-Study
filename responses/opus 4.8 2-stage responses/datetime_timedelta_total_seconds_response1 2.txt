1. **Type consistency**: The output is always a `float`, regardless of the input timedelta.

2. **Equivalence to division**: The output equals `td / timedelta(seconds=1)`, meaning it is mathematically consistent with dividing the duration by a one-second timedelta.

3. **Sign correspondence**: The sign of the output matches the sign of the timedelta — a positive duration yields a positive value, a negative duration yields a negative value, and a zero duration yields `0.0`.

4. **Component reconstruction**: The output equals the sum of the timedelta's components converted to seconds, i.e. `days * 86400 + seconds + microseconds / 1_000_000`.

5. **Negation symmetry**: Negating the timedelta negates the output, i.e. `(-td).total_seconds() == -td.total_seconds()`.
