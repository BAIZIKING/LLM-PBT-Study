## Generation Strategy

To generate a wide variety of inputs and edge cases for `date.isocalendar()`, I'll use Hypothesis's built-in `st.dates()` strategy. This automatically generates `date` objects across the full valid range (from `date.min` = year 1 to `date.max` = year 9999), including edge cases like:
- Year boundaries (Dec 29-31 and Jan 1-4) where the ISO year may differ from the Gregorian year
- Leap years
- The minimum and maximum representable dates
- Mondays (weekday=1) and Sundays (weekday=7)

This gives broad coverage without manually constructing edge cases.

## Properties to Check

Based on the API documentation, I can verify the following invariants:

1. **Return structure**: The result is a named tuple with three components `year`, `week`, and `weekday`.
2. **Weekday range**: The weekday must be between 1 (Monday) and 7 (Sunday) inclusive.
3. **Week range**: The week number must be between 1 and 53 inclusive (ISO years have 52 or 53 weeks).
4. **Year proximity**: The ISO year must be within 1 of the Gregorian year (since only the first/last days of a year can roll into an adjacent ISO year).
5. **Documented examples**: Verify the specific examples from the docs hold (round-trip checks via `date.fromisocalendar` confirming consistency).
